        On behalf of its membership, the Pharmacy Health Information Technology

Collaborative (PHIT) appreciates the opportunity to submit comments regarding the Health
Data, Technology, and Interoperability: ASTP/ONC Deregulatory Actions to Unleash Prosperity
[HTI-5] proposed rule.

       PHIT has been involved with the federal agencies, including the Department of Health

and Human Services (HHS) Assistant Secretary for Technology Policy/Office of the National
Coordinator (ASTP/ONC) and the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS), in
developing the national health information technology (HIT) framework for implementing
secure access of electronic health information to improve health outcomes since 2010.

Pharmacists use health IT, provider directories, telehealth, e-prescribing (eRx), electronic
medical record (EMR)/electronic health record (EHR) systems, and certified EHR technology
(CEHRT) to help manage patients’ health needs. PHIT supports the use of these systems, which
are important to pharmacists in working with other health care providers to deliver longitudinal
and bidirectional person-centered care and guidance on safe medication use. Pharmacists
transmit patient information related to overall patient care, transitions of care, medication lists,
medication allergies, patient problem lists, tobacco use status, and social determinants of
health (SDOH). Pharmacists also use health IT for reporting to public health agencies (e.g.,
immunization reporting), clinical decision support services/knowledge artifacts, checking drug
formularies, and comprehensive medication management (CMM).

Comments

        PHIT has been working with the pharmacy profession to develop baseline electronic

health records (EHR) functionality guidance that aligns with ASTP/ONC certification criteria and
recently published the EHR Certification Criteria Guidance Document for Post-Acute Pharmacy.
This document provides recommendations on ASTP’s Base EHR Certification Requirements and
their applicability for developing pharmacy EHR systems for the purpose of storing and
exchanging patient health information in an interoperable way. Use of health IT for
bidirectional and longitudinal interoperability is critical for pharmacists providing patient-
centered care, especially with the advancements and adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) in
pharmacist clinical care. Pharmacists require seamless access to and the sharing of patient-
specific information. Interoperability, however, is limited within pharmacy practice.

       One reason interoperability is limited in pharmacy practice is that pharmacists were not

included in nor incentivized through the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
Promoting Interoperability Programs (PIPs), formerly the EHR Incentive Programs or
“Meaningful Use,” when it was established in 2009 and formally launched in 2011, as numerous
other health care providers were. To help move interoperability to where it needs to be in
pharmacy practice, PHIT respectfully requests the Secretary include pharmacists in PIPs (see
also PHIT’s February 23 comments on ASTP’s Request for Information: Accelerating the
Adoption and Use of Artificial Intelligence as Part of Clinical Care).

        Overall, PHIT agrees with several of the changes proposed by HTI-5, though not all.

Some proposed reductions to vendor requirements may impact health care providers, including
pharmacists, as health IT vendors and AI vendors may further limit their liability for their
products in regard to privacy, patient safety, security and the content generated, making health
care providers more liable because they are the users providing clinical care. If regulations and
other requirements are removed, PHIT recommends ASTP develop model language for health
care providers to use in vendor contracts so that risks are shared equally. This is addressed
further in one specific area of proposed HTI-5 below (see also PHIT’s February 23 comments on
Request for Information: Accelerating the Adoption and Use of Artificial Intelligence as Part of
Clinical Care).

§ 170.315(b)(9) Care Plan
Comment: PHIT reviewed the current base EHR and agrees with the proposed statement to
remove, as it is not included in the Base EHR definition.

§ 170.315(b)(11) Decision Support Intervention [DSI] and
§ 170.315(c)(1) Clinical Quality Measures – Record and Export
Comment: PHIT reviewed the current base EHR and agrees with the proposed statements to
revise these criteria; however, we also highly recommend that when using evidence-based
medicine with population bias related to pediatrics and geriatrics, clinical decision support
(CDS) include augmented intelligence in the revisions and that ASTP develop model language
for providers to use in contracts to ensure that risks between vendors are shared equally.

        Our concern is that if the required audit/feedback mechanism for DSI is removed,

vendors may not necessarily readjust their algorithms to correct problems (e.g., hallucinations),
as they will no longer be required to check and readjust algorithms, and the problems continue.
DSI is used to ensure patient safety. This is an area in which ASTP can help providers by
developing the model language noted above.

       The Pharmacy HIT Collaborative comprises the major national pharmacy associations,

representing 250,000 members. PHIT’s membership is composed of the key national pharmacy
associations involved in health IT, the National Council for Prescription Drug Programs, and
associate members encompassing e-prescribing, health information networks, transaction
processing networks, pharmacy companies, system vendors, pharmaceutical manufacturers,
and other organizations that support pharmacists’ services.

        As the leading authority in pharmacy health information technology, PHIT’s vision and

mission are to ensure the U.S. health IT infrastructure better enables pharmacists to optimize
person-centered care. Supporting and advancing the use, usability, and interoperability of
health IT by pharmacists for person-centered care, PHIT identifies and voices the health IT
