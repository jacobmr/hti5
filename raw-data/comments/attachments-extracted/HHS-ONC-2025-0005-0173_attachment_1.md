On behalf of the Alliance for Person-Centered Care (the Alliance), we thank you for
providing the opportunity to comment to review and provide feedback on Health Data,
Technology, and Interoperability: ASTP/ONC Deregulatory Actions to Unleash Prosperity
(RIN: 0955-AA09, Docket No. 2025-23896).

The Alliance is a coalition of organizations that are laser-focused on one goal: to expand the
use of patient-reported data in our health care system, through tools such as surveys and
quality measures based on patient-reported data. Patient-reported data are demonstrated
to have valuable uses for patients and providers but often is difficult for patients to submit
to their providers and/or is not interoperable among providers on their care team. Ideally,
patient-reported data (such as goals, symptoms, pain levels, etc.) should be submitted
once by a patient and easily shareable across health systems and providers as part of a
patient’s medical record. The critical objectives, as it relates to patient-reported data, are:

       a) making it easy for patients to provide information,
       b) minimizing the number of times that patients have to provide the same
       information,
       c) providing that information to all interested parties that could take advantage of
       knowing patient goals, symptoms, etc., and

d) ensuring that the insights from these data are available to patients and families to
use in managing their health and health care as well as to clinicians and care teams
supporting them.

Digital, interoperable data can make all of these objectives possible.

We commend ASTP/ONC for proposing signiﬁcant deregulatory actions that reduce burden
while advancing interoperability, including removal of outdated certiﬁcation criteria and the
clear articulation of an "America's FHIR®-Forward Future" policy.

Further, we encourage ASTP/ONC to ensure that these changes will make it easier for
patient-reported data to be collected, used and shared.

We offer several speciﬁc comments:

• We call on ASTP/ONC to align the Certiﬁed Electronic Health Record technology
(CERHT) program to interoperability principles by certifying HL7 FHIR APIs that
providers and payers receive. This can allow health care organizations to more
easily use technology outside of the EHR for functions related to collecting, using
and sharing patient-reported data. This will make it easier to use technologies that
have consumer friendly tools for capturing and using data and that can more easily
allow for functions that support patients and their care teams in guiding and
tracking outcomes.
• We urge ASTP/ONC to revise § 170.315(c)(3) to permit technology-neutral quality
reporting through either QRDA or FHIR®-based implementation guides, mirroring the
regulatory structure already established for electronic case reporting. This will
advance the adoption of digital quality measures, including measures that use
patient-reported data.
• We support proposed changes that will strengthen the rules seeking to limit
information blocking. Currently, these exceptions can be used to limit data sharing
in unnecessary ways, therefore creating barriers to quality measurement and
innovation. Speciﬁcally, we support removal of the TEFCA Manner Exception and
the Third-Party Seeking Modiﬁcation Use Condition, revision or removal of the
Manner Exception Exhausted Condition.

Finally, the Alliance is excited that ASTP/ONC’s work through this and upcoming rules will
enhance the relevance and actionability of health data and prioritize ease of use for
clinicians and care teams as well as patients and families. We encourage ASTP/ONC to
