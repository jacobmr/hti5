Re: Comments to “Health Data, Technology, and Interoperability: ASTP/ONC Deregulatory
Actions To Unleash Prosperity”

Rocky Mountain Equality appreciates the opportunity to comment in response to the HHS Assistant
Secretary for Technology Policy’s public comment period regarding deregulation of health IT.

Rocky Mountain Equality rejects the ASTP’s underlying premise that deregulating electronic health
record companies will somehow lead to “prosperity.” In fact, the removal of more than half of the
required data fields in electronic health record systems will lead us backwards in time to when medical
records couldn’t be seamlessly transferred between providers because the data fields didn’t match up.

For LGBTQ+ patients specifically, this deregulation is harmful both by intent and impact. HHS proposes
to remove critical data fields that are essential to delivering high quality care for the LGBTQ+ patient
population. These data fields include “name to use”, “sex parameters for clinical use”, “sexual
orientation”, “gender identity”, and “pronouns.”

When these data fields are required to be included in electronic health records, it means that patient
records can be transferred between providers in a more seamless way. This isn’t woke or divisive. It’s an
essential function of healthcare delivery.

We strongly urge the ASTP to not undermine the health and well-being of LGBTQ+ Americans and leave
the regulatory requirements for these data fields in place.
