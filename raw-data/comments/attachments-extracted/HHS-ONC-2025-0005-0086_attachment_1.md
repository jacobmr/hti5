Dear Assistant Secretary and ONC Leadership:

On behalf of the Physical Activity Alliance (PAA), the nation’s leading coalition focused on
advancing evidence-based policy and systems changes that support physical activity for all
Americans, we appreciate the opportunity to provide comments on ASTP/ONC’s Health
Data, Technology, and Interoperability proposed rule.

Primary Recommendation

We urge ASTP/ONC to adopt USCDI v4 in §170.213 in place of USCDI v3.1 to ensure
inclusion of physical activity assessment within certified electronic health record
technology.

The physical activity assessment was added to USCDI v4 in 2023 and has been included in
subsequent versions. Adoption of USCDI v4 is essential to enable standardized capture,
exchange, and use of physical activity data within certified health IT. Without inclusion in
the adopted USCDI version, physical activity cannot be fully integrated into certified
electronic health record workflows, limiting its interoperability and scalability across
health systems.

Alignment with CMS and CMMI Policy

Advancing to USCDI v4 is directly aligned with recent CMS and CMMI actions promoting
prevention and lifestyle-based care. CMS has formally recognized physical activity
assessment as a reimbursable clinical service in the 2026 Physician Fee Schedule (HCPCS
Code G0136), and CMMI’s MAHA ELEVATE Model requires incorporation of physical
activity or nutrition as core intervention components.

To fully operationalize these payment and innovation models, health systems must be able
to capture and exchange standardized physical activity data. Adoption of USCDI v4 will
ensure that certified health IT supports this federal policy direction and facilitates
consistent implementation across care settings.

Implementation Infrastructure Is Already in Place

PAA, in partnership with the American Heart Association, coalition partners, leading
experts, and a technical consultant, collaborated through HL7 to develop the first HL7 Fast
Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) Physical Activity Implementation Guide (IG),
Version 1.

This Implementation Guide establishes a standardized, evidence-based framework for
integrating physical activity into clinical care, including assessment, prescription, referral,
and follow-up consistent with the Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans, 2nd edition. It
enables efficient capture of structured physical activity data within electronic health
records and supports interoperability through FHIR-based exchange.

Adoption of USCDI v4 would complement this existing infrastructure and accelerate
nationwide implementation of standardized physical activity data exchange. We also
encourage ONC to consider incentivizing adoption of the Physical Activity Implementation
Guide within certification criteria to support consistent implementation across certified
health IT products.

Importance for Population Health and Cost Containment

Physical activity is one of the most effective and cost-efficient interventions available for
improving health outcomes. Extensive evidence demonstrates that regular physical activity
reduces the risk of at least 25 chronic conditions, including cardiovascular disease, type 2
diabetes, several cancers, depression, and anxiety. Adults who are physically active have a
20–30 percent lower risk of premature death.

Beyond disease prevention, physical activity improves functional status, cognitive health,
mental well-being, and quality of life—outcomes directly relevant to value-based care and
Medicare sustainability.

Ensuring that physical activity data can be measured, standardized, exchanged, and acted
upon within health care systems is therefore essential to advancing national goals related
to chronic disease prevention, treatment, and health care cost containment.

Conclusion

For these reasons, we strongly urge ASTP/ONC to adopt USCDI v4 in §170.213 and to
support certification pathways that enable implementation of standardized physical
activity data exchange consistent with federal payment and innovation policies.

We appreciate ASTP/ONC’s leadership in strengthening the nation’s health data ecosystem
and stand ready to assist in advancing interoperable solutions that integrate physical
activity into routine clinical care.
