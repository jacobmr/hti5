Point-of-Care Partners is an employee-owned health IT consulting firm with deep expertise in health data standards, value-based care and digital transformation. We offer actionable strategies, program guidance and implementation support to optimize interoperability and information management in a complex, rapidly evolving healthcare ecosystem.

POCP supports ASTP/ONC&rsquo;s goals and appreciates the opportunity to comment on the HTI-5 proposed rule. Our aim here is to provide experience- and evidence-based insights, analysis and recommendations to help HTI-5 achieve its intended outcomes without shifting cost, risk or operational burden onto providers, payers and implementers.

To ensure that deregulatory actions fuel the growth and effectiveness of healthcare interoperability, POCP broadly recommends that the HTI-5 final rule:

Prevent burden shifting and preserve accountability for displaced safeguards.

-Maintain a clear interoperability floor during the transition to APIs.

-Make API requirements executable through clear scope and conformance.

-Ensure information blocking rules are administrable for automated exchange.

-Preserve multi-path exchange (including TEFCA) without creating a ceiling.

-Embed security, identity, and consent as core API requirements.

-Retain guardrails for AI-enabled DSI to protect transparency and access to EHI.

-Provide a phased, testable implementation timeline aligned with HTI-6.

Recommendations based on real-world experience relating to cost, risk and scalability across payer, provider and vendor workflows. Together, they identify where HTI-5 can most-effectively bolster interoperability.

Prevent Burden Shifting and Preserve Operational Predictability

-Pair removed/revised requirements with clear responsibility assignment, crosswalks, and post-final-rule evaluation.

-Assign displaced safeguards and capabilities to Health IT developers.

-Preserve developer testing as data exchange is now more network-to-network and automated.

-Avoid ambiguity that results in ad hoc integrations or one-off arrangements.

Preserve an Interoperability Floor During the API Transition

- Define a minimum interoperability floor for all certified products.

- Outline the path for replacing certification criteria with FHIR-based API requirements.

-Continue support for multistakeholder standards development (e.g., HL7 Da Vinci, FAST).

-Avoid single-connection or bilateral integration models impeding scalable exchange.

-Make API Requirements Executable

-Clearly scope API requirements and conformance expectations.

-Do not require support for undefined future capabilities.

-Promote predictable, reusable patterns to reduce custom integration burden.

-Make Information-Blocking Rules Administrable and Enforceable

-Provide practical examples and workable documentation expectations.

-Curb procedural and strategic obstacles to automated, standards-based exchange.

-Align enforcement expectations with machine-to-machine access patterns.

-Preserve Multi-Path Exchange (Including TEFCA)

-Enable TEFCA where appropriate without making it a ceiling for API-based exchange.

-Clarify expectations for TEFCA participants while supporting API-forward workflows.

-Encourage technically superior or operationally appropriate exchange mechanisms.

-Security of Health Information and Trust

-Embed interoperable authentication/authorization/identity/consent approaches.

-Operationalize HL7 FAST IGs for security, identity and testing at scale.

-Streamline certification to reduce fragmentation and build cross-sector trust.

-Certification and Decision-Support Interventions

-Retain governance and transparency for AI-enabled DSIs.

-Ensure testing scenarios reflect actual clinical and operational risk.

-Prevent DSIs from interfering with EHI access for patient care.

Implementation and Timing

-Publish a phased transition timeline with clear implementation milestones.

-Align HTI-5 and HTI-6 to minimize uncertainty and duplicative investment.

POCP serves as Program Managers for multistakeholder initiatives, including HL7 Da Vinci and FAST. Our experience implementing digital health standards across diverse environments allows us to assess HTI-5&rsquo;s real-world impacts across stakeholders.

POCP encourages continued collaboration between ASTP/ONC, healthcare organizations, health plans, technology vendors, and other stakeholders to accelerate progress. Working together, we can unlock the full potential of health data exchange, enabling providers to make well-informed decisions, empowering patients to actively participate in their care and, ultimately, leading to a more efficient, effective system for all.

We welcome further discussion on our recommendations.
