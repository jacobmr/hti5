# Comment: HHS-ONC-2025-0005-0071

**Commenter:** Lauge Sokol-Hessner

**Posted:** 2026-02-24T05:00:00Z

**Source:** https://www.regulations.gov/comment/HHS-ONC-2025-0005-0071

---

As a practicing physician, I strongly urge ONC/ASTP to retain federal certification criteria requiring usability testing and safety-focused design practices for electronic health records (EHRs).

EHR usability is not an abstract concern, it is a patient safety issue. There is substantial evidence that poorly designed interfaces -- including confusing displays of information and sub-optimally designed workflows -- contribute to medication errors, incorrect orders, documentation mistakes, and diagnostic delays. The preventable harm that follows is inexcusable.

Certification requirements that include structured usability testing and safety practices are not administrative luxuries; they are safeguards that protect patients and clinicians. Eliminating these requirements in the name of burden reduction risks increasing downstream burden in the form of adverse events, rework, malpractice exposure, and clinician burnout.

Safety and burden reduction are not mutually exclusive. Thoughtful usability standards can and should advance both. Other high-risk industries maintain long-standing safety-oriented design requirements because the stakes demand it. Healthcare should be no different.

I respectfully urge ONC/ASTP to preserve usability and safety-focused certification requirements to ensure that EHR systems support, rather than undermine, safe patient care.
