Preamble, Section I.A.2.b &ldquo;America&rsquo;s FHIR-Forward Future&rdquo;
InterSystems supports ASTP/ONC&rsquo;s direction to prioritize HL7 FHIR-based exchange as the foundation for scalable, automated interoperability. We share ASTP/ONC&rsquo;s objective to modernize exchange beyond document-centric paradigms, and we agree that FHIR&rsquo;s resource-based model enables more granular, discrete data exchange than document-centric approaches such as C-CDA, supporting workflow automation, decision support, analytics, and longitudinal record assembly without forcing implementers into the ambiguity of document parsing.
InterSystems encourages ASTP/ONC to emphasize that a &ldquo;FHIR-forward&rdquo; approach should advance computable interoperability - where exchanged data is reliably structured, attributable, and usable - not simply accessible. We believe this will reduce integration burden, improve data quality, and accelerate innovation across care delivery, payer, public health, and third-party application ecosystems.
Preamble, Section I.A.2.b &ldquo;America&rsquo;s FHIR-Forward Future&rdquo; AI-enabled interoperability (FHIR + MCP): trust, security, and governance
InterSystems appreciates ASTP/ONC&rsquo;s recognition that AI-enabled interoperability may emerge through approaches that combine FHIR-based exchange with newer standards such as Model Context Protocol (MCP).
InterSystems recommends that ASTP/ONC frame MCP-like approaches as adjacent to, not a substitute for, core interoperability standards (FHIR/USCDI). AI context-sharing mechanisms should not bypass established requirements for standardized data representation, secure access controls, and operational accountability.

To support safe, scalable AI-enabled interoperability, InterSystems recommends guardrails such as: (a) strong authentication/authorization and least-privilege access; (b) provenance and traceability for source data and AI outputs; (c) auditability/logging appropriate to clinical risk; and (d) governance expectations that discourage proprietary &lsquo;AI-only&rsquo; side channels that fragment exchange.

Part 170 discussion, Section III.A.2.a Care Coordination Certification Criteria, &ldquo;Transitions of Care&rdquo;
InterSystems supports ASTP/ONC&rsquo;s continued movement toward FHIR-based, discrete interoperability. However, we recommend ASTP/ONC reconsider the proposed revisions to 170.315(b)(1) that would remove the ability to &ldquo;create&rdquo; and to &ldquo;send&rdquo; transitions-of-care C-CDA documents and narrow certification primarily to &ldquo;receive and validate.&rdquo;
Transitions of care remain a core, high-volume exchange workflow across heterogeneous settings, and document-based exchange will continue in the near term - particularly across cross-vendor transitions and long-term/post-acute care. If certification no longer covers create/send capabilities, implementation consistency may degrade and shift integration burden downstream to providers, HIE/HIN operators, and recipients.
InterSystems recommends ASTP/ONC maintain a baseline, end-to-end transitions-of-care capability (create/send and receive/validate) during the transition, while continuing to advance FHIR-based alternatives through discrete, testable implementation guidance.
