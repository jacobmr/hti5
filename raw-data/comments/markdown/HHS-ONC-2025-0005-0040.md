Re: Comments to &ldquo;Health Data, Technology, and Interoperability: ASTP/ONC Deregulatory Actions To Unleash Prosperity&rdquo;

Middlesex Health wishes to express our support for the proposed revisions to the definitions of &ldquo;access&rdquo; and &ldquo;use&rdquo; of Electronic Health Information (EHI) under 45 CFR 171.102, which explicitly include automation technologies such as robotic process automation (RPA) and autonomous artificial intelligence systems (Agentic AI).

This regulatory clarity is crucial for the future of health information technology and patient care. The explicit codification that &ldquo;access&rdquo; and &ldquo;use&rdquo; apply to automated means will achieve several key objectives:

- Provide Essential Regulatory Clarity: By explicitly naming RPA and Agentic AI, the proposal removes ambiguity, allowing hospitals and developers to invest confidently in and deploy these transformative technologies to improve efficiency and enhance the delivery of care without fear of inadvertently violating information blocking regulations.

- Strengthen Anti-Information Blocking Efforts: The proposal reinforces that actors who interfere with the automated access, exchange, or use of EHI may be engaging in information blocking. This protection is vital to ensure that AI systems, which rely on seamless, automated access to EHI for their operational integrity, can function as intended to support clinical and administrative workflows.

- Promote Health System Efficiency: As the rule acknowledges, the increased use of automated means is key to improving efficiency. Establishing a clear framework for automated data use will accelerate the adoption of AI and RPA solutions that can drive down costs and reduce administrative burden.

These proposed revisions are a necessary and timely update to ensure that the spirit of the Cures Act, which promotes the seamless access and use of health data, is upheld in the age of advanced automation and artificial intelligence.
