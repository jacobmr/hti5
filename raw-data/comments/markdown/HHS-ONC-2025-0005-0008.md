Comment Title:
Support for Agile-Driven, FHIR-Based Interoperability and Reduced Certification Burden

Comment:
Trilogy Innovations, Inc., an SBA 8(a) Certified Small Disadvantaged Business supporting mission-critical systems for federal agencies including the FBI, DOJ, and BOP, strongly supports the proposed rule (RIN 0955&ndash;AA09) issued by ASTP/ONC. This deregulatory initiative enables greater agility, innovation, and interoperability across the federal health IT landscape.

As Prime on the FBI NCIC Modernization contract (FAST CLIN 3), Trilogy is modernizing legacy mainframe infrastructure into a secure, event-driven microservices architecture using cloud-native technologies and SAFe Agile. We believe this rule&rsquo;s emphasis on FHIR-based standards and streamlined certification aligns perfectly with the operational demands of secure, high-availability environments.

We particularly commend the removal of outdated, low-value certification criteria, which too often impose administrative burdens without advancing technical outcomes. The ability to reallocate that time and budget toward innovation and mission impact will improve service delivery across government.

Finally, we encourage ONC to reinforce acquisition strategies that reward technical realism and proven delivery models. Trilogy&rsquo;s recent GAO-validated contract win over a lower-priced competitor shows that the government is best served by firms who offer credible staffing, agile frameworks, and operational continuity over merely low-cost proposals.

We appreciate this opportunity to comment and support ASTP/ONC&rsquo;s efforts to enable secure, scalable, and interoperable health IT systems.
