Dear Sirs and Madams:

I am an attorney who deals daily with electronic medical records, including audit data. We attempt to obtain, on behalf of our clients (patients and their families), the truth as to what happened.

It is already not easy with the current rules and regulations.

I have learned of HTI-5 and am very concerned as to what such deregulatory changes could mean in the search for the truth.

Medical records are already not being preserved or are being changed and deleted in attempts from preventing the patient or their family from finding out what actually happened.

Please pause HTI-5 and ensure that the proper steps, protocols, and enforcements are in place to ensure the integrity of patients medical records.

Regards,

Megan J. Frantz Oldham
