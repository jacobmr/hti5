To the Assistant Secretary for Technology Policy and the Office of the National Coordinator for Health IT,

I am writing to express my strong opposition to several key provisions in the proposed rule, "Health Data, Technology, and Interoperability: ASTP/ONC Deregulatory Actions to Unleash Prosperity." While I support the goal of reducing administrative burden, the proposed removal of essential certification criteria threatens the safety, security, and equity of our national health IT infrastructure.

Specifically, I oppose the following actions:

Removal of Certification for Clinical Decision Support (CDS): Removing oversight for how technology assists clinicians in making life-saving decisions&mdash;including the removal of "model cards" for AI transparency&mdash;is a step backward. In an era of rapid AI integration, we need more transparency into algorithmic logic and bias, not less.

Elimination of Patient Safety and User-Centered Design Requirements: Rescinding requirements for safety-enhanced design (SED) shifts the burden of catching technology-induced errors onto overstressed healthcare providers. This increases the risk of medical errors and clinician burnout.

Rollback of Privacy and Security Guardrails: The proposal to remove or descope criteria related to multi-factor authentication (MFA), audit reports, and encryption is deeply concerning given the rise in healthcare cybersecurity breaches. Reducing federal security standards leaves patient data more vulnerable.

Reduction in Demographic Data Capture: I oppose the removal of standardized data elements for sex, sexual orientation, and gender identity. Comprehensive data is essential for identifying and addressing health disparities; removing these requirements hampers public health efforts and harms marginalized communities.

Conclusion: The proposed "reset" of the Certification Program prioritizes developer convenience over patient safety and data integrity. I urge the ASTP/ONC to maintain these critical guardrails to ensure that health IT remains a safe and reliable foundation for American healthcare.
