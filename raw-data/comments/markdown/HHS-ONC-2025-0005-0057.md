On behalf of Orion Heath, I am responding to the Assistant Secretary for Technology Policy (ASTP)/Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology (ONC) (collectively, ASTP/ONC), Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) Heath Data, Technology, and Interoperability (HTI-5): ASTP/ONC Deregulatory Actions to Unleash Prosperity proposed rule published in the December 29, 2025 Federal Register.

Orion Health, a HEALWELL AI company, is a global healthcare technology firm specializing in interoperability, health data infrastructure, and population-level data integration. In the United States, Orion Health partners with state governments, health systems, health information exchanges, clinically integrated networks, and risk-bearing entities to enable secure data exchange, improve care coordination, and support value-based care initiatives.

With more than 30 years of experience supporting large-scale health data platforms globally, Orion Health brings an infrastructure-first perspective to operationalizing health IT standards at scale. Our solutions are designed to connect disparate data sources, support governance and data quality, and help organizations translate data access into operational readiness across clinical, administrative, and public health use cases.

---

Orion Health supports the objectives of HTI-5 to reduce unnecessary administrative burden across the healthcare system. We agree that simplification is needed; however, it must not come at the expense of interoperability as a core, enforceable requirement. Interoperability must remain a non-negotiable foundation of the health IT ecosystem.

Drawing on our experience supporting health data exchange across regional and national communities, we believe burden reduction efforts should focus on eliminating duplicative documentation and manual workflows, rather than weakening the technical standards that enable meaningful interoperability in practice. FHIR-based APIs should remain the default mechanism for health information exchange, supporting timely, secure, and scalable data sharing across care delivery, public health, and community-based partners. Interoperability should be measurable in performance, reliability, and real-world usability, not declared through API availability.

As certification requirements are streamlined, corresponding expectations for vendor accountability must remain clear. Health IT developers should be required to demonstrate real, testable interoperability in production environments, maintain strong privacy and security protections, avoid information blocking, and ensure that information sharing remains the default unless a legitimate legal, safety, or privacy concern applies. Moving beyond read-only exchange toward automation-ready interoperability will be essential to achieving the efficiency gains envisioned by this rule.

Where HTI-5 introduces or enables AI-supported capabilities, transparency is essential. Vendors should clearly communicate the purpose, intended use, and limitations of AI systems, particularly when such technologies are used to support clinical efficiency or decision-making. Clear disclosure is necessary to support trust, appropriate use, and regulatory oversight. Responsible AI deployment depends on complete, timely, and standardized data flows; interoperability is therefore a prerequisite to safe and scalable AI adoption.

HTI-5 represents an important opportunity to reduce costs while improving the usability, reliability, and trustworthiness of health data. Orion Health supports reforms that accelerate innovation while preserving interoperability as a foundational requirement, and we appreciate the opportunity to provide feedback as ASTP/ONC advances this framework.
