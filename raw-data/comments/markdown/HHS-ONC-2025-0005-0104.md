Please see the attached letter with our comments on the ASTP/ONC Deregulatory Actions to Unleash Prosperity HHS-ONC-2025-0005, HHS-ONC-2025-0005-0001, 2025-23896

The health centers we represent rely extensively on Certified Electronic Health Record Technology (CEHRT) and interconnected health information systems to deliver care across multi-site environments. Most operate with lean IT teams and limited in-house cybersecurity expertise, making vendor-based certification safeguards a critical component of their HIPAA Security Rule compliance strategy. Our member centers routinely manage sensitive protected health information across cloud-hosted platforms, revenue cycle vendors, pharmacy interfaces, health information exchanges, and third-party population health tools, creating a complex and highly interdependent digital environment.

Given this distributed technology architecture, the removal of federal privacy and security certification criteria would not reduce compliance obligations for health centers; rather, it would shift technical validation, due diligence, and risk assessment responsibilities onto organizations that do not control software design or development. For safety net providers operating on narrow margins, the loss of certification &ldquo;floors&rdquo; materially increases operational risk, financial exposure, and vulnerability to cybersecurity threats
