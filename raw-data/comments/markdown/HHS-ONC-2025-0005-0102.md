# Comment: HHS-ONC-2025-0005-0102

**Title:** Comment on HHS-ONC-2025-0005, HHS-ONC-2025-0005-0001, 2025-23896

**Commenter:** Anonymous Anonymous

**Posted:** 2026-02-25T05:00:00Z

**Source:** https://www.regulations.gov/comment/HHS-ONC-2025-0005-0102

---

The retention of accurate data is critically important. Retention policies must be clearly defined to ensure that information remains accessible for the duration of patient care, investigations, or litigation holds, and to maintain full compliance with applicable state and federal regulations.
We strongly urge HHS to prohibit the use of &ldquo;ephemeral messaging&rdquo; or any system that permits automatic deletion of data. All data must be preserved in a readable and reproducible format.
We support the development of clear, uniform definitions for terms such as ePHI, EHI, Generative AI, and Synthetic Data. However, before any deregulation of certification is considered, data preservation standards &mdash; including requirements for reproducibility &mdash; must first be modernized, codified, and meaningfully enforced. Eliminating the existing framework without implementing essential safeguards would perpetuate unsafe practices and facilitate improper billing conduct. Sections 170.315(d)(10), 170.315(d)(2), 170.315(d)(3), 170.315(b)(11), and 170.315(g)(3) should not be removed.
Robust audit trails are fundamental to patient safety and system accountability. In the absence of clearly defined and enforceable standards, the integrity of electronic health information cannot be assured. This gap would expose patients to significant clinical risk and leave taxpayers vulnerable to fraud and abuse.
