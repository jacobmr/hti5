Thank you for recognizing the importance of all of the current EHR certification criteria. You point out that their ubiquity shows their value and you're confident that this value alone is sufficient to ensure new EHRs will continue to utilize them even if not required. As a practicing physician I have appreciated how these features have helped me, my colleagues, and my patients to enjoy the benefits of a comprehensive, coordinated, secure, and efficient healthcare ecosystem.

I also appreciate the importance of reducing barriers to new EHR vendors and innovation, and can understand that extensive certification programs introduce friction.

So I find it mind-boggling that your solution to this conundrum is to remove certification criteria instead of making them easier to certify. It is my proposal that any criteria that you intend to remove, simply be shifted to attestation. That is zero certification burden but ensures what you had only hoped: that EHR vendors would continue to implement those critically important features.
