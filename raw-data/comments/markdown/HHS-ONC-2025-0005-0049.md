De-regulation tends to be a slippery slope and considering the frequency of data breaches that continue even at this late date I don't think that not putting certain block measures in place is a smart move. This is about protecting people's privacy and as someone who has unfortunately experienced multiple medical breaches I feel very strongly about this both as a patient and as employee of multiple medical facilities. And like people in real life, AI can be influenced by bad data as evidenced by chatbots on various platforms: racist rhetoric, hate speech and degradation of women are just a few offending topics that were featured prominently.

In short, the system needs to implement more stringent measures to protect information not loosen the reins so to speak

Deregulation is the reduction or elimination of government rules, laws, and restrictions imposed on businesses and industries. It aims to promote competition, lower consumer prices, increase efficiency, and encourage innovation by reducing compliance burdens. While it often leads to lower prices and more choices, it can also cause market instability, monopolies, and reduced safety standards.
