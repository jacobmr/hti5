Hi ASTP team,
I&rsquo;m writing as a 35-year HIT industry person and as a patient, and I&rsquo;m honestly worried about the direction of the HTI 5 proposal. I&rsquo;m all for better interoperability, but changing certification rules before updating the basic standards that keep electronic health data safe feels backwards.
Audit trails are what make a medical record trustworthy, and the current standard is outdated. Healthcare has changed a ton since 2018&mdash;AI tools, device integrations, messaging systems, and more. If we loosen requirements now without modernizing how data is tracked and stored, we&rsquo;re setting ourselves up for mistakes, safety issues, and even fraud.
We really need a new audit trail standard that:
&bull;Shows what AI tools and devices did and when.
&bull;Identifies which software was used.
&bull;Captures detailed activity like edits, copy paste, and user roles.
&bull;Makes sure patients can actually access their own records.
And we need clear rules about keeping data. Auto deleting messages or letting information disappear is dangerous. Data has to stick around in a readable format for as long as it&rsquo;s needed for care, investigations, or legal reasons.
Before we change certification rules, we need updated definitions and updated data preservation standards. Otherwise, we&rsquo;re removing guardrails without putting better ones in place.
Audit trails aren&rsquo;t optional&mdash;they&rsquo;re the foundation of trust and accountability. I&rsquo;m asking HHS to pause the proposed changes until a stronger, modern standard is ready.
Thanks for reading,
