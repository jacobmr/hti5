# Comment: HHS-ONC-2025-0005-0208

Submission Pillar 1: Domestic Sovereignty for Critical Infrastructure "As HTI-5 eliminates 70% of prescriptive certification, I recommend that ASTP establish a 'Domestic Ownership Standard' for all Jurisdictional Hubs receiving federal infrastructure funds. To safeguard national security and align with state-level protections like the Lone Star Infrastructure laws, entities managing the primary data 'pipe' for a state should be 100% U.S.-owned and operated without collaboration with a foreign company. > Furthermore, in alignment with EO 14192 (Unleashing Prosperity through Deregulation) and the Cyber Incident Reporting for Critical Infrastructure Act (CIRCIA), ASTP should establish a 'Domestic-Control Standard' for entities serving as Jurisdictional Hubs. As we move toward AI-enabled interoperability, the entities managing the 'Data Pipe' for state-wide exchange must be U.S.-owned and operated to prevent foreign-parent interference in critical health infrastructure."

Pillar 2: The HDU as the "Manner Exception" Safety Net "I support the removal of the TEFCA Manner Exception but recommend that ASTP replace it with a 'State-Designated Utility (HDU) Safe Harbor.' Small rural hospitals cannot manage the technical risk of autonomous AI (as defined in HTI-5) on their own. I recommend that ASTP explicitly recognize State-Designated Health Data Utilities (HDUs) as the primary facilitators of the 'TEFCA Manner Exception' for Information Blocking. Designating an HDU provides these providers with a 'Technical Safe Harbor,' ensuring federal compliance while reducing administrative burden."

Pillar 3: Protecting Federal Grants (RHTP Alignment) "I recommend that any state receiving Rural Health Transformation Program (RHTP) grants utilize a 'CMS-Aligned Network' model. This prevents the fragmentation of data and ensures that federal funds are spent on public-interest utilities rather than proprietary, foreign-controlled silos. As HTI-5 expands the scope of 'Access' to include autonomous AI systems, I recommend that such access be managed through Non-Profit Jurisdictional Utilities to ensure the data used for AI-enabled clinical efficiency is subject to public-interest governance."

These comments represent my individual expert perspective as a member of HITAC and a health technology leader, and do not necessarily represent the consensus view of the full committee or my peers.
