Dear ASTP representatives:

I am an attorney who has been practicing law in Montana for forty-nine years. During most of my career, I have represented hospitals, physicians, and patients in medical malpractice litigation. Over the past ten years, healthcare information has moved almost exclusively to electronic records. Obtaining complete medical records is very difficult. The only means I have to confirm that I have all of the records is by comparing the computer system audit trail with the records provided. All too often the audit trail reveals undisclosed records.

A complete audit trail is vital to assuring that providers and patients are able to understand what, when, why, how and by whom events occurred. Protecting the integrity of patients' medical records and making audit trails easily accessed are essential for all.

I urge you to continue protecting the requirements for complete audit trails and access to them.
