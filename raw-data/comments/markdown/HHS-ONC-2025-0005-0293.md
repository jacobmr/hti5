Population-based cancer surveillance provides unbiased, representative cancer statistics for monitoring cancer occurrence and trends across all populations in the US. It is the backbone for cancer control and public health policy and assessments. Every state in the US has a state law mandating cancer reporting that need to follow data reporting standards set by the North American Associations of Central Cancer Registries (NAACCR) that serves as the standard setter for North America, working collaboratively with the stakeholders and support from the cancer registry community. Standards are important to be consistently followed and updated in order to ensure the data quality and sound analyses. The HTI-5 proposed deregulation measures risk the derailment of the existing NAACCR standards. Therefore, I fully support the analysis and recommended revision as submitted by NAACCR.
