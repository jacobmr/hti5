Dear Dr. Keane,

Fairview Health Services (Fairview) appreciates the opportunity to provide comments on the Assistant Secretary for Technology Policy/Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology (ASTP/ONC) Deregulatory Actions to Unleash Prosperity (HTI-5) Proposed Rule.

Fairview is a nonprofit, integrated health system providing exceptional health care across the full spectrum of medical services through our 10 hospitals and 60 clinics. Fairview&rsquo;s broad care continuum includes academic and community hospitals, primary and specialty care clinics, senior and long-term care facilities, retail and specialty pharmacies, pharmacy benefit management services, rehabilitation centers, counseling, home health care services, and an integrated provider network. Fairview&rsquo;s 34,000+ employees and 5,000+ system providers embrace innovation and new thinking to drive a healthier future through healing, discovery, and education.

Fairview values ONC&rsquo;s focus on reducing administrative burden, strengthening interoperability, and modernizing the regulatory foundation to support Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) based Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) and AI enabled data exchange. We offer the following feedback grounded in our operational experience, technology ecosystem, and previous comments on ONC rulemaking. Fairview appreciates ONC&rsquo;s continued efforts to reduce administrative burden by building on its action to address multiagency reporting burden, improving coordination across Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) and Qualified Health Information Network (QHIN) ecosystems, and eliminating workflow burdens needed to achieve better treatment-oriented data transfer between health systems. We request that ONC designate resources to help organizations become part of a QHIN, provide appropriate QHIN oversite, require QHIN&rsquo;s to share data effectively in a standardized way, and require EHR vendors to take that data and show it to physicians and care givers in a way that is consistent with their workflow. This will greatly enhance the ability for patients to navigate health systems to get optimal care with efficiency for providers. ONC should also structure the Trusted Exchange Framework and Common Agreement (TEFCA) network in a way that QHIN&rsquo;s can help with federal and state public reporting for regulatory purposes and share standardized quality data as the next goals for interoperability through TEFCA. The most dangerous elements from this proposed deregulation are:
&bull;Loss of AI transparency artifacts (model cards) leaving a large burden on health systems with significant inefficiency and risk to determine safety and quality of AI tools
&bull;High risk longitudinal data like family history, problem lists, and social data not having appropriate standards for dates and clinical context causing inaccurate AI interpretations and clinical errors due to poor quality data and potentially issues with patient care if the information is not standard functionality
&bull;Erosion of certified clinical decision support (CDS) to place most of the burden on provider organizations and not EHR-vendors. Also, there is a need to ensure that providers and other clinicians stay in the loop for designing what things can be autonomous and what things need clinical oversight with AI guidance instead of being fully autonomous
&bull;Transfer of AI safety accountability to providers and health systems without continued regulatory oversite of vendor AI development through FDA and other regulatory mechanisms
&bull;Potential abuse of individual patient data by opening access to non-provider organization entities without HIPAA regulation and decreasing oversight
&bull;Erosion of cybersecurity standards including removal of encryption standards for authentication credentials and other standards which will put health systems at risk for cybersecurity attacks and data leakage

Thank you for consideration of Fairview&rsquo;s comments on the HTI-5 proposed rule. Fairview values ONC&rsquo;s focus on reducing administrative burden, strengthening interoperability, and modernizing the regulatory foundation to support FHIR-based APIs and AI-enabled data exchange.
